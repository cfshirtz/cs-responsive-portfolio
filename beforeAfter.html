"Before and After" Process: Pink Floyd's "Animals"

Original version of Pink Floyd's 1977 album "Animals"

The original album cover is timeless, replete with industrial-capitalist symbolism. However, what if we imagined packaging a new, remastered version that keeps the essence of the original, but shaves off some of the grainy harshness.

Start from scratch. Build shapes with rectange tool and re-use essential color palatte with eyedroper tool.

Background Color. Experimenting with smoke stack flow on the right. This helps maintain eyeline (horizon) from this point foward.

Building colors established, some line and shape detail missing. Experimenting with clouds. Pen tool has been used to great effect by creating smoke stacks. Gradient mesh is used heavily at this point.

Removed clouds, looks much better. The cleaner look compliments the "refreshed" approach to a re-master album.

"Letter Box" lines at the top and bottom help clean up and hide unruly minutae of the bottom. Also eliminates extra space at top.


"Before and After" Process: Red Rocks Poster Card

Red Rocks

Red Rocks Photograph taken by me in 2010 before a concert.

Basic lines and shapes traced through Procreate tablet drawing program.

Adding frames and text through Adobe Illustrator. Black and White version could possibly stand on it's own (in my opinion).

Transferred back into Procreate tablet drawing program. Added color with eyedropper tool (using original photo for reference), then increasing opacity - giving the piece a pastel-pallette quality.
